## Why This Exists

Linear notebooks hide relationships, and standalone flashcard apps forget where a fact came from. Students retype the same concept into Anki while the original lecture note drifts out of date. **RemNote** exists to keep knowledge and memory practice in one graph: write the idea once, reference it everywhere it matters, and schedule reviews without maintaining a second disconnected deck file.

**RemNote** solves **one specific problem**: connecting durable notes with spaced repetition so recall practice stays tied to the explanations you wrote from courses, books, and licensed study resources you already use.

If exams, certifications, or long-term mastery depend on remembering what you studied, **RemNote** is for you. Once rem references and card queues feel natural, review becomes a continuation of note-taking rather than a separate chore.

## Quick Start with RemNote

1. **Get RemNote** — Create an account or open the desktop client and sign in to your workspace.
2. **Launch** — Start a top-level document for the course or topic you are actively studying.
3. **Write rems** — Capture ideas as bullets; nest details under parents so structure stays clear in **RemNote**.
4. **Link concepts** — Reference earlier rems instead of duplicating definitions across documents.
5. **Make cards** — Convert key lines into flashcards or cloze deletions while the context remains visible.
6. **Review (optional)** — Open the practice queue and rate recall so **RemNote** schedules the next interval.
7. **Work normally** — Return daily; new notes feed the same graph, and **RemNote** keeps due cards waiting.

## Understanding RemNote Core Components

**RemNote** merges a networked outliner with a spaced-repetition engine:

- **Rems and documents** — Atomic ideas living inside hierarchical pages you organize in **RemNote**.
- **References and portals** — Reuse a rem in multiple places without losing a single source of truth.
- **Flashcards and clozes** — Generate review items directly from the notes you already trust.
- **Spaced repetition queue** — Schedules practice based on how well you recalled each card in **RemNote**.
- **Daily documents** — Lightweight capture surfaces that later sort into lasting structure.
- **Search and backlinks** — Find every place a concept appears when exams demand synthesis.
- **PDF learning tools** — Work beside study files you are allowed to store within **RemNote**.
- **Descriptors and templates** — Standardize how definitions, theorems, or cases are recorded.
- **Knowledge bases** — Separate school, work, and hobbies without mixing review queues accidentally.
- **Document hierarchy** — Nest topics under parents so large courses stay browsable inside **RemNote**.

These pieces keep **RemNote** centered on learning material you create or are licensed to study.
